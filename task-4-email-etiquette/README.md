Effective digital communication through email can prevent misunderstandings and potential serious consequences. For example, if a student sends an email to their instructor in an informal tone, without a subject line, or without clarity in the message itself, it is very possible that the instructor could misconstrue or potentially even choose not to respond to an email like that. This scenario could lead to missed deadlines and lost opportunities for the student.

Respectful email etiquette should be followed to avoid these types of situations. This includes using a clear subject line, greeting the recipient in a respectful manner, structuring the email in a professional way, and closing the email with a polite and respectful closing. Additionally, proofreading the message prior to sending it will ensure that it has clarity and professionalism when it is received. Responsible and respectful effective digital communication is very important in academics and professional environments.
